Manager, Programs & Community Growth

at The Kindness Campaign
Posted today $60,000/year Full Time Hybrid · Downtown Austin Verified nonprofit

Requirements

Preferred Experience

  • 5+ years in program management, community engagement, education, nonprofit leadership, partnerships, events, or operations
  • Experience managing projects or initiatives from concept to execution
  • Experience working with schools, volunteers, partners, or community organizations
  • Strong operational and organizational skills
  • Experience building systems, processes, or scalable programs preferred
  • Familiarity with AI tools, automation, or modern operational systems

Bonus Points For

  • Startup or growth-stage experience
  • Volunteer management experience
  • Experience with experiential programming or community-building

Description

About the Role

This is not a traditional nonprofit coordinator role. We're looking for a builder — someone who can help take ideas and turn them into scalable programs, systems, experiences, partnerships, and community initiatives.

This role will help support and grow TKC's programming, community engagement, volunteer experiences, partnerships, and future initiatives as the organization continues to expand nationally. The ideal candidate is highly organized, strategic, creative, emotionally intelligent, and excited to help build the future of TKC.

What You'll Do

  • Help manage and grow TKC programs and community experiences
  • Build systems, playbooks, and processes
  • Lead and own volunteer and community engagement initiatives
  • Coordinate with schools, partners, sponsors, volunteers, and community organizations
  • Support events, activations, and organizational initiatives
  • Help develop and launch new ideas and opportunities for growth
  • Identify ways to improve scalability, efficiency, and impact

What Success Looks Like

  • Launch a scalable program playbook within your first 90 days in two or more emerging markets that any future TKC team member or partner can run
  • Own and grow the volunteer program, turning one-off volunteer moments into a repeatable engagement engine
  • Deepen and grow school and community partnerships — hitting 80–90% partnership retention and 25% revenue growth from existing accounts

Who You Are

You're probably already doing a version of this job somewhere else — just without the mission, the runway, or the permission to build something that matters.

You want a role where the ceiling is high, the work is visible, and what you build actually reaches kids, families, and communities. You see kindness not as a slogan, but as a discipline.

To apply: Send your CV, cover letter, and answer two short prompts in your application about translating a vision into operational reality and a community or program you've built or grown.

Salary & Benefits

Starting base salary: $60,000 annually

You'll Also Receive

  • 100% employer-paid health, dental, vision, and life insurance beginning the 1st of the month after your start date
  • Observance of NYSE trading holidays, plus the day after Thanksgiving
  • Up to 6 weeks of PTO annually after 6 months of full-time employment
  • 401(k) plan (no employer matching at this time)

You'll Have the Chance To

  • Create meaningful national impact
  • Help shape the future growth of TKC
  • Build programs and experiences that change lives
  • Work directly with leadership and grow alongside a rapidly expanding organization

Company

The Kindness Campaign is a nationally growing nonprofit focused on emotional health, emotional literacy, and community impact for children, teens, families, schools, and communities.

TKC creates experiences, programs, tools, and partnerships that help people build emotional health skills for life.
